About us

Witkop Hunting Safaris

We offer plains game and dangerous game hunting, featuring a diverse range of hunting packages across South Africa, designed to provide an exceptional hunting experience. We consistently adhere to ethical hunting techniques at all times. Through selective hunting, these areas consistently yield high-quality trophy animals.

The bushveld around the Springbok Flats in Limpopo makes for a memorable hunting destination with a variety of game, including Impala, Blesbuck, Red Hartebeest, Blue Wildebeest, Zebra, Sable, Nyala, Kudu, Cape Buffalo, and much more.

Our game farm, with its breathtaking sunrises and sunsets, surrounded by beautiful marula trees, creates a truly picturesque landscape in the bushveld.

We offer tours to two of the most beautiful sceneries in South Africa: the Garden Route and the Kruger National Park. Additionally, there are other activities available, such as Adventure with Elephants, Crocodile tours and feedings, Ziplining, and the Eventiera Wildlife Park, among others.

Our team is well-trained and skilled in their respective fields, contributing significantly to the day-to-day operations of Witkop Hunting Safaris. With their respect and friendliness, they create a pleasant atmosphere and working environment.

Come and enjoy the magnificent bushveld for a once-in-a-lifetime African hunting experience. I, Wessel van der Walt, a Hunting Outfitter and Professional Hunter, will personally guide you on your hunting expedition, ensuring you derive much pleasure and create unforgettable memories.

This is where the safari starts and friendships are made. Become part of the adventure and book with us.

Wessel van der Walt Hunting Outfitter &
Professional Hunter

My passion for hunting in this beautiful bushveld and its wildlife began with my father, "Barefoot Teunis," a farmer and an exceptional hunter, my idol, living on the edge of the Springbok Flats near Tuinplaas, Limpopo.

My father is a phenomenal stalker in the bush because he never wears shoes; his feet are as tough as elephant hide, allowing him to move soundlessly through the veld. One of my favorite hunting stories involves him stalking and ultimately shooting an elusive big male leopard that had been killing our cattle and calves for months.

The first time my father took me hunting was in the summer of 1998 on the farm Mackenzie. We stalked bush pigs feasting in our corn fields, as these creatures are notorious destroyers of South African corn fields. During the stalk, "Barefoot" stopped me and whispered softly, "Make ready." He placed the rifle on his shoulder and instructed me, "Take him when you are ready." I took the shot and killed my first bush pig at the age of 5, a truly memorable day with my father that set me on the path to my professional hunter and outfitter career.

In 2014, I met my wife, Mellissa, and fell head over heels for her. She is a skilled huntress and fishing enthusiast, perfectly aligned with my line of work. "Love her very much." In 2015, she assisted me in building and developing our game farm and hunting camp. We established our hunting business, Witkop Hunting Safaris, in 2016, fulfilling our dream and passion every day. While we began by hunting with South African biltong hunters, our big dream was about to come true: welcoming clientele from all over the world to hunt and experience South Africa and all nature can offer on our farm, Witkop Hunting Safaris. It is the final piece of the puzzle.

We are looking forward to meet you, and become friends to guide you through your dream hunting adventure.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Q: What rifle caliber is suitable for my hunting safari?

    A: For pursuing plains game, opting for the .30 caliber proves to be an excellent choice given its widespread popularity and effectiveness. When it comes to potentially dangerous game, a caliber of .375 H&H or larger is recommended for enhanced safety.

    Prepare for your plains game hunt by bringing a minimum of 40-60 rounds of ammunition. Exceptional bullet options include the Nosler Partition, Trophy Bonded Bear Claw, and the Barnes TSX (or TTSX).

    If your target is the Cape Buffalo, ensure you bring 60 rounds of ammunition – 40 premium soft points and 20 non-expanding bullets (solids). It is crucial to test your loads beforehand to confirm that both solids and soft point bullets share the same point of impact. The Hornady DGX/DGS combination comes highly recommended, and the Barnes TSX also stands out as an excellent choice for this formidable game.

  • Q: What documentation is required for bringing my own rifle?

    A: We offer complimentary support in obtaining a temporary firearm import permit and will assist you in smoothly navigating the Customs process for your rifle upon arrival in South Africa, all at no additional cost.

  • Q: If I choose not to bring my own rifle, can you supply one for me?

    A: We offer the rental of rifles along with appropriate ammunition for hunting plains game or Cape Buffalo, as needed.

  • Q: Can I opt to rent a rifle for my Safari?

    A: Approximately 60% of our clients choose this option, primarily to avoid the complexities of traveling internationally with their own rifles. If your route involves a stop in London, where transportation regulations can be more cost-effective, bringing a rifle may not be feasible, making rifle rental an attractive alternative.

    We offer high-quality scoped rifles in various calibers for hire, including .308 Howa, .30-06 Vektor, .308 Norma Magnum, and .375 H&H. Notably, both the .308 Howa and .30-06 Vektor are equipped with suppressors. The rental fees are $50 per day for the rifle and $5 per round for ammunition.

  • Q: What is the cost of airfaire to South Africa?

    A: The price of round-trip economy class airline tickets from the United States to Johannesburg (JNB) typically ranges from $1,000 to $2,200, depending on your departure location and whether you choose a direct or connecting flight. These rates are standard during the peak season from May through August, with the possibility of finding more affordable tickets outside of this timeframe.

    For those departing from the United States, Delta Airlines offers a direct flight from Atlanta to Johannesburg. Additionally, South African Airways provides direct flights to Johannesburg from Washington Dulles and JFK. We highly recommend considering these flights, especially for American hunters intending to bring their own rifles.

  • Q: Where will will I be received at the airport?

    A: Your Professional Hunter (PH) will greet you at O.R. Tambo International Airport in Johannesburg immediately after your Customs clearance. If you are bringing your own rifle, your PH will accompany you to the South African Police Service office to assist in the retrieval process. Before your hunt, we will provide you with comprehensive instructions on navigating the airport, complete with photographs indicating the meeting point and identifying your PH.

  • Q: Can I bring a non-hunting guest with me?

    A: Certainly! Our facilities in South Africa are exceptionally comfortable, and we extend a warm welcome to non-hunting guests in our camp. Many of our hunters bring along friends and/or family members who may not share an interest in hunting but still wish to experience the wonders of Africa. Consequently, non-hunting guests are encouraged to join the hunters on safari each day, and we are delighted to organize game drives or other wildlife viewing opportunities for their enjoyment.

  • Q: Do I need to be concerned about any diseases?

    A: We advise consulting your doctor before your hunt and informing them of your travel destination. To participate in a hunt in Limpopo Province, it is necessary to take a malaria prophylaxis. Additionally, it is advisable to receive a Tetanus booster before your departure. It's worth noting that South Africa is free from Yellow Fever and Ebola.

  • Q: What is the culinary experience like in Africa? Will I have the opportunity to enjoy the game I hunt?

    A: Certainly, you will have the chance to savor the animals you hunt. African wild game is known for its delectable taste, and our experienced cook adeptly prepares delightful meals using the hunted animals. While it's not possible to take the meat home, any surplus at the conclusion of your hunt is either consumed by the camp staff or donated to those in need, ensuring no wastage. All our meals are freshly prepared on-site, providing three hearty meals a day along with snacks. You won't go hungry during your hunt! Our beverage offerings include coffee, tea, bottled water, soda, juice, beer, wine, and liquor. Breakfast typically includes coffee, rusks (hard, dry biscuits), cereal, fruit, and juice. Lunch and dinner feature a variety of bread, meat, vegetables, and fruit, capped off with evening desserts. If you have any allergies or dietary restrictions, please inform us before your hunt so that we can make necessary preparations.

  • Q: What are the accommodations like in Africa?

    A: Our hunters are accommodated in individual chalets, each equipped with air conditioning and Wi-Fi. Each chalet is meticulously maintained and includes a private bathroom. The camp also boasts a well-stocked bar and an outdoor fire pit.

  • Q: Can I hunt additional animals?

    A: Certainly! It's highly likely that you'll encounter various species during your safari that were not initially part of your planned hunt (some of which you might not have been aware of before arriving in Africa). We prioritize flexibility, and if you decide to pursue additional animals, simply inform your Professional Hunter (PH). They will make every effort to provide you with an opportunity to take the desired additional animals. The pricing for extra animals follows our standard trophy fees.

  • Q: What is the success rate of obtaining all the animals inclued in my package?

    A: While it is exceptionally rare, there have been instances where a hunting client did not get an opportunity at all the animals included in their package. We engage in year-round game scouting to maximize our clients' chances of harvesting the animals on their list within the designated package. Certain hunts may involve methods such as sitting in a blind, stalking, or utilizing a spotlight at night, but rest assured that we commit 100% effort to make your hunting goals a reality.

  • Q: What happens if I wound an animal and do not recover it?

    A: Despite our utmost efforts to retrieve all animals wounded during our hunts, any animal that is injured and cannot be located will be charged at the full price.

  • Q: How can I arrange for the shipment of my trophies back home?

    A: At the lodge, we will professionally skin and salt your capes according to your preferences, whether it be for a Life Size, Shoulder Mount, Rug, etc. Subsequently, we will handle the transportation of your trophies to Nico van Rooyen Taxidermist. They will undertake tasks such as dipping, boiling the skulls, cleaning horns, crate preparation, and processing the necessary export paperwork. An invoice for these services will be sent to you via email approximately 1-2 months later.

    Once the crate is ready, it will be picked up by an export agent, who will then provide you with a quote for shipping back to your home country, typically via Air Freight. It is advisable to engage an import agent in your home country to receive the crate with your trophies. They can then arrange for its direct delivery to your home through Ground freight.

  • Q: How many sets of clothing should I pack?

    A: With daily laundry service available at our lodge, there is no need to bring more than 3-4 sets of safari clothes.

  • Q: When is the hunting season?

    A: Our peak season spans from late February to mid-October, with the most pleasant weather occurring during the winter months from May to early September. While hunting is available throughout the year, it's important to note that temperatures can rise significantly from late October to late February, bringing hotter conditions.

  • Q: Will my phone work, and do you have Wi-Fi?

    A: For mobile phone usage, contact your service provider before departure to ensure international service availability. Wi-Fi is accessible at the lodge, although it may be intermittent (typical for the region). While your mobile phone should function in most areas, we recommend turning off your data to avoid excessive data rate charges.

  • Q: Are there any specific medical fitness requirements for participating in a hunting safari with Witkop Hunting Safaris?

    A: Participants are generally expected to be in good physical health. Kindly disclose any pre-existing medical conditions, and we'll tailor the safari to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for you.
